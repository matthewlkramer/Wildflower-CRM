Highest impact
1. Tame the dashboard's wall of numbers. It currently shows 20 metric tiles (10 for FY2026, 10 for FY2027, split across Revenue/Gifts and Loan Capital). That's a lot to scan, and the Loan Capital rows are almost all "$0 / —", so they add noise without insight. Consider: lead with one hero number (progress to goal as a visual bar — "$3.33M of $4M, 83%"), collapse FY2027 and empty Loan Capital sections behind a toggle, and let the user pick the default fiscal year. A single progress bar communicates "are we on track" faster than five tiles do.
2. Reduce empty-column clutter in list views. On Individuals, columns like Priority tier, Status, Capacity, Lifetime giving, and Last gift are mostly em-dashes. Rows of dashes train the eye to ignore the table. Either default to a tighter column set (Name, Last contacted, Active funders, Owner) and let people add columns, or visually de-emphasize empty cells. The Columns picker already exists (13/20) — make the default leaner.
3. Speed up perceived load. Nearly every page flashes "Loading…" then skeleton bars then content — Individuals took ~4s. Even if the data fetch can't be faster, keeping the table headers/filters painted instantly (you partly do this) and showing skeleton rows immediately rather than a "Loading…" text flash will feel much snappier.
Role-tailoring (you mentioned fundraisers, admin, finance)
4. Give each role a different home and trimmed nav. Right now everyone sees the same 18-item sidebar. A fundraiser rarely needs Reconciliation, Donorbox Review, Financial Corrections, or Audit Log; finance lives in those. Consider role-based defaults: fundraisers land on Top Priorities or their own pipeline; finance lands on Reconciliation. Collapse the Admin and Finance sidebar groups by default for fundraiser accounts. This shrinks the cognitive load without removing access.
5. Make "what's mine vs. the team's" a first-class filter. Owner columns and "My/Team upcoming meetings" already exist — surfacing a persistent "My records" toggle near the top would help fundraisers cut 3,000+ individuals down to their book instantly.
Detail & form polish
6. The contact detail page is dense — add a sticky action bar. It's a strong 3-column layout, but the most common actions (Log interaction, Add task, Add note) sit mid-page. A slim sticky header with the person's name + primary actions would keep them reachable while scrolling. Also, the many inline edit pencils create visual busyness — consider revealing them on row-hover rather than always-on.
7. Surface the kanban as the default for Opportunities. Your pipeline board (Cold lead → Written commitment, with column totals) is the most intuitive view in the app and exactly how fundraisers think. Consider making it the default rather than the list, with drag-to-advance-stage if not already supported.
Smaller wins
Make the global search (Ctrl-K) more visually prominent — it's the fastest path through 3,000 records but currently reads as a faint placeholder. Add empty-state guidance ("No open opportunities — add one") with an inline action instead of just text. And ensure consistent terminology between "Funder/Donor/Organization" which currently appear somewhat interchangeably.
